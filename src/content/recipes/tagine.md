## Ingredients


### Tagine

- 1 eggplant
- 2-3 stalks celery
- 2 carrots
- 2 medium onion, diced
- 6–8 garlic cloves, chopped
- 60 g raisins
- 100 g dried chickpeas (soak overnight and cook them)
    - _cook with a garlic clove and bay leave brings more aroma_
- 400 g tomato paste or diced tomatoes
- 250 ml chicken broth
- 1 tbsp, ras el hanout
    - 1 tsp cumin
    - 1 tsp coriander
    - 1/2 tsp cinnamon
    - 1/4 tsp turmeric
    - 1/4 tsp ginger
    - 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
    - 1/8 tsp cloves
    - 1/8 tsp cardamom
    - 1/8 tsp nutmeg
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp coriander
- 1/4 tsp cinnamon
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp pepper
- _toasted almonds can be a nice crunchy kick_

### Quinoa

- 1 1/2 cup quinoa
- 3 cup chicken broth
- 1 small cinnamon stick or 1/2 teaspoon powder
- 1/2 tsp cumin
- 1/2 tsp salt, more to taste
- 1/2 tsp garlic paste
- 1–2 tbsp olive oil
- _fresh chopped herbs like cilantro or parsley can be a nice add on_

## Description


### Tagine

1. Cut the eggplant into small chunks, salt them and let them sit for 20 minutes. Rinse and pat dry.
2. In a pan roast them with olive oil until golden. Set it aside.
    1. For deeper flavour, roast the eggplant in the oven at 200°C with olive oil until caramelised
3. Preheat the oven to 180°C
4. In the same pan heat olive oil and sauté the onions and garlic until
5. Add celery and carrots and cook until tender about 10-15 mins
6. Lower the heat and add the spices. Stir well and allow spices to enhance their aroma.
7. Add tomato and chicken broth. Bring to simmer.
8. Add the chickpeas and raisins and let everything simmer for another 10-15 mins.
9. Nestle in the eggplant and drizzle some olive oil.
10. Cover with a lid and place it in the oven for 30 mins.

### Quinoa

1. bring broth and all the spices to boil
2. lower heat and add the quinoa
3. cover and let it cook until no more water
