## Ingredients

- 70 g Flaxseeds (Linseeds)
- 60 g Sesame Seeds
- 60 g Sunflower Seeds
- 50 g Chia Seeds
- 50 g Pepitas
- 300 g filtered Water
- 20 g Extra virgin Olive Oil
- 1/2 tsp sea salt

### Optional Flavor Variations

- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp Paprika
- 1 tsp Oregano

## Instructions

1. **Preheat** oven to 160°C (320°F).
2. **Combine** all seeds in a bowl and pulse in a food processor or blender briefly to break them up slightly (don't over-mill).
3. **Add** water, olive oil, salt, and any chosen spices. Blend again just to mix.
4. **Let the mixture sit** for about 5 minutes to allow the chia and flax to gel and bind the batter.
5. **Spread** the batter thinly and evenly over two lined baking trays. Aim for a consistent thickness for even baking.
6. **Bake** for about 1 hour, or until golden and crisp. Rotate trays halfway through if needed.
7. **Cool completely**, then break into pieces and store in an airtight container.

## **Nutty Variations**


### 1. **Almond-Crusted Crackers**

- **Add** 50–70 g _chopped or slivered almonds_ (or almond meal for smoother texture).
- Optional: Add a pinch of **rosemary** or **thyme**.
- Notes: Almonds toast well, so keep an eye on them during baking to avoid burning.

### 2. **Walnut & Rosemary Crackers**

- Replace sunflower seeds with **crushed walnuts** (60 g).
- Add 1 tsp **chopped fresh rosemary**.
- A touch of **black pepper** works well here.

### 3. **Hazelnut & Cumin Crackers**

- Add 40–50 g chopped **toasted hazelnuts**.
- Add ½ tsp **ground cumin** and ½ tsp **smoked paprika**.
- Sweet-salty vibe: try adding a small pinch of **maple syrup** or **date powder**.

## **Sweet-ish Variations (Snackable!)**


### 6. **Cinnamon Almond Crunch**

- Add 1 tbsp **maple syrup** or **date syrup**.
- Add ½ tsp **cinnamon**.
- Include 40 g finely chopped **almonds**.
- Sprinkle with **sesame seeds** before baking.

### 7. **Coconut & Seed Delight**

- Replace 20 g of seeds with **unsweetened shredded coconut**.
- Add 1 tsp **vanilla extract**.
- Optional: Add a pinch of sea salt and a tiny bit of **coconut sugar**.
